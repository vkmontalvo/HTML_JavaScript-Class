My Hobbies



Did you know that working out can be just as beneficial for you mind as is it for your body?

It's true!

Science has proven that exercise releases endorphins, which result in a feeling of happiness and euphoria. This can combat depression, anxiety and low self-esteem!

Not to mention it promotes creativity, mental clarity and blood flow to your organs, including your brain.

After a long day--and a good workout--there's nothing quite like curling up with a good book and a hot beverage.

Besides being both relaxing and beneficial to your vocabulary, books can also be a great stress reliever and boost your IQ and creativity.

Plus, they're a great alternative to television nowadays (Kardashians, anyone?).

If all else fails, there's always video games.

Fun and somewhat addictive, play video games can actually be beneficial too. No, really!

Video games can actually be mentally stimulating, allowing the player to improve at solving puzzles, planning and, of course, hand-eye coordination.

Not to mention shooting a few zombies is a great way to relieve stress.
